    1. Relationships with parents and families give pre-teens and teenagers emotional support, security and safety.
    2. Parent's support helps pre-teens and teenagers navigate the ups and downs of adolescence.

Teens and Romantic Relationships Do's and Don'ts

  • Do look for someone you feel comfortable with
    Being comfortable with someone means:
    1. You can be yourself around her.
    2. You can have different opinions on something, and know that it’s okay.
    3. You trust each other when you’re not together.

  • Don’t forget your friends
    Some people will drop all their friends after they start dating someone. They might not mean for it to happen, but it still does. Don’t be that person! No one wants a friend who will throw her over for someone else, and you still need a social life outside your boyfriend or girlfriend.

  • Do know the difference between good and bad conflict
    We tend to think of conflict as a bad thing, but it isn’t always. Conflict can even bring a couple closer together if they are able to stick to these rules during a disagreement:
    1. Explain how you feel and be specific
    2. Listen to how she feels and try to be understanding
    3. Don’t bring up past disagreements

Counselling for teens and dating

  • Childhood and adolescence is one of the most important stages of development in a human. During their growing years, children and teens face a lot of physical, psychological, identity and relationship changes that are a part of their journey to adulthood. This time can be difficult and at times be overwhelming and while it is normal, some children need help in navigating through it and coming our strong and balanced to take on adult life. Counselling helps teenagers and their families to identify ways to understand and walk this journey in a way that encourages the child’s psycho-social development. Counseling helps teenagers to be safe, feel confident about themselves while engaging and building respectful relationships. It helps children with relationship concerns, social and relationship adjustment, Behavioral concerns, etc.
